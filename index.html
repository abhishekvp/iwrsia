About the Workshop

Remote sensing is now heading towards providing quantitative answers to questions from the user community who are no longer satisfied with qualitative analyses. In order to provide quantitative and accurate information from remotely sensed images, it is essential to make comprehensive models of the imaging systems, medium, and target so that the received remotely sensed data can be properly understood and interpreted. This involves solving complex theoritical models as demonstrated in some of the very recent papers in international journals of repute. The speakers in the Workshop are expected to be carrying out pioneering work in applied theoritical model for remote sensing and understanding of Planet Earth, and the participants are highly likely to benefit from attending their presentations and exposure to the applications and case studies.

The workshop will allow participants to share state of the art knowledge on a variety of topics of common interest in the field of image processing, pattern recognition and machine learning with particular attention to remote sensing data. It will enable discussions and open opportunities for joint investigations in the future. Discussions among participants will allow a broadening of scope, thinking and allow exploration of lateral schools of thought.

Content Overview

The workshop would cover the following topics:

  • Active Remote Sensing (SAR, LiDAR, SONAR)
  • Passive Remote Sensing
  • Classification, Regression, Content Based Information Retrieval
  • Analysis of Multispectral Data
  • Analysis of Multitemporal Data

A one day hands-on tutorial has been planned for the first day for students pursuing Masters and PhD studies in the field of remote sensing image analysis. The tutorials will contain from basics to advanced concepts in image processing of remotely sensed images with a glimpse into the recent developments in this field. The lecture notes/slides will be distributed to the participants at the end of the tutorials. The lectures will be conducted by experts from India and Italy.
